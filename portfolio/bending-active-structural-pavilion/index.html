    Pavilion

    Form-Finding methods based on Bending-Active structures


  • The goal of my research is to achieve a double-curved prototype in building-scale which is easily assembled and uses material efficiently. The thesis provides a geometric strategy which integrates computational design, advanced fabrication methods and materials research.


  • Bending-Acive Structural Pavilion

  • As a designer and researcher, I propose the problem statement is how to design and fabricate a structural double-curved building-scale prototype based on materiality.

  • Building-Scale Prototype

  • Through extensive present of curvature in the building materials, the pavilion achieves its signature shape. The process is based on maximizing the overall double curvature of a surface to create its volume and take advantage of its structural capacities.

  • Detail

  • With computational design and optimized digital fabrication workflows, the time of assembly is reduced and material is used efficiently. The resulting pavilion maximizes structural stress.

  • Assembly Drawing

  • The Bending-Active Structural Pavilion measuring 2.75 meters in height and 2.5x2.67 square meter. According to its lightweight structure, I prefabricate and ship it from my university to the exhibition.

  • I install the lights inside pipe structure of pavilion and shows its spatial experience.
