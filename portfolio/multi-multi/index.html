  • Multi-Multi Language Application

    • Challenge

      How to engage with the individual learning pattern and help learners to practice languages?

      Solution

      Design an app that will improve learning quality and help people practice the languages through the understanding of different cultures and exploration of attractions around the world.

  • Persona

  • Understanding multilingual learners and users who are the first time learning a foreign language.

      Alex is a busy IT consultant who speaks multilingual languages. He needs to practice the language and experience different cultures because he can travel and communicate without the language barrier.

      Joan is a tidy housewife who learning her first foreign language. She needs to practice the language and explore something new at her odd moment because she can travel to different countries and visit her children abroad.

  • Storyboard

  • 1.One day, when users study and practice languages. 2.They think “I will travel to Switzerland and speak in German.” 3.Users open the “Multi-Multi” application. 4. Search the place they want to visit.

    5.The user searches for "Zurich ice cream shop". There is a dialogue on the screen showing how to order ice cream in German. 6.Users explore different sights and practice languages on the Multi-Multi application. 7.Users practice the language by exploring the world. 8.They have learned multiple languages and have a better understanding of different cultures.

  • Information Architecture & User Flow

  • Here I work on designing a responsive website and mobile application. I use the Multi-Multi sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen’s design to ensure a cohesive and consistent experience across devices.

    The purpose of user flow is to provide users with appropriate materials to practice language and learn more about local culture. According to the city or attraction the user is looking for, the app provides vocabulary, conversation, reading ,and listening.

  • Usability Study

  • I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

    • Insight

      Basic learning materials


      People search for more materials on the Internet to practice listening, reading, speaking, and writing.

      Search function


      Users find where they want to visit and plan their trips easily by directly searching for attractions.

      Add to favorites


      People want to add different categories to the collection as their notes. With this feature, users also can practice languages and prepare conversations for their trips.

  • Main Features

  • Practice the languages with attractions

    • Users explore sights and practice relative vocabulary. Search some restaurants and learn the basic conversation to order your food.

    • Also can read the introduction of the attraction and the origin of the event.

  • Make your notes and save your trips

    • Take notes about vocabulary, conversations, and introductions by saving sights to your list. You can also plan your trip to practice your language.

  • Reflection

  • I have always been a self-taught person, learning different skills from coding and robotics to languages. When studying in Switzerland, the first step in learning German was to go to the supermarket to learn food names. Learn from the little thing I do twice a week. These learning experiences inspired the idea of designing this language application. Establishing connections with good experiences in life is a great help in practicing a language. Most people like to travel the world. I took advantage of traveling to design a learning environment that allows users to practice vocabulary and dialogue when planning a trip. When users are on a trip, they can have small conversations easily and naturally. I hope I can launch this application as one of my side projects and use this product to help more people learn.
