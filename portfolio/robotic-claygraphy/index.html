    Exploring the Tool-path for Carving Clay Material Utilising Robotic Movement


  • This thesis presents the exploration of carving textures and patterns on clay models utilising the complex gestures of robotic movements. This thesis presents the exploration of carving textures and patterns on clay models utilising the complex gestures of robotic movements.


  • Robotic Claygraphy

  • The fabrication parameters are discovered through a series of basic experiments. After discovery, we generate the tool-path from the curvature of our target geometry in order to carve out clay material. With this workflow, we fabricate different carving effects utilising robotic movement.

  • Robotic Claygraphy
    Overview

  • To generate the whole tool path with continuous coordinate frames, the carving process has different constraints from the afore-mentioned pick-and-place robotic control. These constraints are given by the tool orientation and the configuration of the 6 joints on the robotic arm.

  • Generate the Whole Tool-Path

  • We apply this technique of fabrication to prototype 2.5D surfaces and 3D objects. It further shows the potential in subtractive robotic fabrication on clay material.
